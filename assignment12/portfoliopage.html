my portfolio.

This is my portfolio page showcasing some of my favorite pieces from one of my classes this semester at Illinois State. I begin with my most recent pieces and then some projects from the last three years. Each have a specific purpose, a special story, or simply no story at all, just experiments with new mediums of art. Take a scroll, if you are so bold, and message me with any thoughts you have on my work!

pentax photography.

This semester I took a Darkroom Photography class and one of the requirements was that I needed to purchase a Pentax K1000 manual focus camera for the first assignment of the course. I was very excited to be able to use this camera because in the past I had only worked with a digital camera. The experience of using a vintage film camera was very humbling in comparison to using digital. We had to shoot two rolls of film for this project and edit our best ones in Photoshop to print out. I’ll admit it was a bit scary taking photos and not being able to see how they turned out until developed, but I think that it also limited me in a good way. By not knowing the outcome I had to believe that the photos were good, which gave me more options for scenery to capture. Here are the best photos from my film.

lumen prints.

There are aspects of this course that I was not expecting. When I think of a Darkroom class I think about how I see them in movies or tv shows, however it wasn’t all about being in the darkroom or shooting film. One of the things I learned about was a form of camera-less photography developed in the 1830s by William Talbot, called lumen prints. It is the process of placing an object onto sensitized paper and exposing it in natural sunlight. One thing my professor provided for us was a light box, which is like a frame that flattens the object onto the paper creating a more contrasted result. I really enjoyed this project because depending on the exposure time the prints could create some really warm colors. Here six of my best lumen prints!
